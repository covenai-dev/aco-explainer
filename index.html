What is ACO?

Agent Citability Optimisation is the practice of making your website structured, trustworthy, and accessible enough that AI agents — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini — will cite it, link to it, and return it as a result when users ask relevant questions.

ACO vs SEO

SEO optimises for search engine rankings. ACO optimises for AI agent citations. They share some foundations but diverge significantly in what actually moves the needle.

Common questions

What site owners ask when they first encounter ACO.

Do I need to change my content to improve my ACO score?

Usually not dramatically. Most ACO improvements are structural — adding schema markup, fixing your robots.txt, adding an llms.txt file, and ensuring your pages have clear author and date metadata. Content quality matters too, but the technical layer is where most sites have quick wins.

Does this replace SEO?

No — ACO and SEO are complementary. A well-structured, trustworthy site ranks well in both traditional search and agent citation. But the optimisation signals diverge: keyword density matters less to an AI agent than factual accuracy and clear information hierarchy.

How do AI agents decide what to cite?

Each model has its own approach, but the common factors are: clear authorship, factual specificity, recency signals, domain trust, and structural clarity. Sites that make it easy for agents to extract precise, verifiable information get cited more frequently.

What is the Agent Citation Index (ACI)?

The ACI is CoveN AI's ranked index of which sites AI agents are actually citing across categories. It's the output signal — ACO is the inputs (what you optimise), ACI is the outcome (whether it worked). The index launches once scan volume makes it statistically meaningful.

Quick wins for any site

The highest-impact ACO improvements, ranked by effort vs score gain.

  • 1.Add an llms.txt file — a plain-text index at /llms.txt that tells agents what your site covers. Low effort, high signal.
  • 2.Explicitly allow AI crawlers in robots.txt — GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, and 5 others. Blocking any removes you from that model's citation pool entirely.
  • 3.Add datePublished and dateModified — undated content is deprioritised by every major AI model. Add it to schema.org markup and as a visible <time> element.
  • 4.Wrap content in semantic HTML — use <article>, <section>, and <main> so agents can extract discrete content blocks independently.
  • 5.Add specific, checkable data — statistics, percentages, and measurable claims increase citation probability 3.1× over general prose.
